Where are the smart cities?

Smart cities are municipal entities that incorporate information and communications technologies into city management. Though they are gaining popularity as solutions to urban sustainability, the complex implications that emerged from the planning, design, and construction of smart cities are far from clear. The goal of Smart City Tracker is to lay the foundation for understanding the impact of the smart city trend on urban sustainability. Smart City Tracker is an interactive, web-based, and open-source platform where users can track and explore local governmentsâ€™ smart city implementation in the United States. It uses Google Custom Search API to search smart city keywords on official city websites. The current version covers 322 cities with a population of more than 100,000. It will be scaled up to cover all 19,519 municipalities including both metropolises and small towns if it receives external funding. The map and dataset are updated periodically, documenting a timeline of implementation, and eventually serves as a living archive of smart city prevalence.
